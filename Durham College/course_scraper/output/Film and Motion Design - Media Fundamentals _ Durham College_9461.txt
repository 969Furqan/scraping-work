Program Overview
Unlock your creative potential with our comprehensive two-term program in film and motion design. This program introduces students to a variety of media, including animation, graphic design, video, motion graphics, and digital production workflow. Gain essential creative and technical skills to produce
captivating animated
content for diverse industries. Build a standout portfolio for potential entry to advanced Faculty of Media, Art & Design programs, including
Animation – Digital Production
,
Game – Art
,
Graphic Design
,
Interactive Media Design
and more.
This program is perfect for anyone looking to enhance their capabilities in content creation and building accessible digital assets.

Program Details
In this program, students work with various visual media such as photographs, video, animation, and motion graphics. The curriculum covers the history of motion, image
sequencing, holography, 3D printing technique
s
, video production and computer animation.
Students gain hands-on experience with
cutting-edge
technologies and processes, giving them insight into five key programs within the Faculty of Media, Art & Design: Animation – Digital Production, Game – Art, Graphic Design, Interactive Media Design, and Video Production. This program is designed to provide students with essential skills and prepare them for specialized study in these exciting fields. Students will build a diverse portfolio
showcasing
their technical and artistic abilities, which is perfect for applications to advanced programs.
PROGRAM LEARNING OUTCOMES
Choose appropriate tools, techniques and applications to create visual narratives and presentations.
Discuss how animation, film and other forms of visual motion styles integrated elements of the social and physical world.
Create designs and visual storyboards by applying critical thinking and problem-solving skills to stage a balanced composition.
Create stop-motion films and motion graphic assets by applying the principles of animation.
Analyze the social and technical effectiveness of narrative forms when used on different media platforms.
Utilize a variety of industry standard audio/video software applications and techniques in the creation of media for a wide range of platforms.
Develop an education and career plan within the range of career options.

Meet Your Faculty Experts
K. Jennifer Bedford
Faculty Member, Faculty of Media, Art & Design (MAD)
Jennifer Bedford is a program and research coordinator at DC. Her lens-based work
has been published internationally and exhibited across North America, Europe, and Africa. She holds a Master of Arts in Communication and New Media and an honours undergraduate degree in Studio Art and Multimedia from McMaster University.
Shaun Feldman
Faculty Member, Faculty of Media, Art & Design (MAD)
With over eight years of experience in graphic design, motion design, brand development and an MA - Design, Shaun specializes in creating impactful visual identities and campaigns for national brands and broadcasts. He leverages industry expertise to deliver innovative, real-world design education.
Kevin D. Fraser
Faculty Member, Faculty of Media, Art & Design (MAD)
Kevin D. Fraser is an award-winning, internationally screened documentary filmmaker. A former TV writer, Kevin has worked on over a dozen different TV shows, from live events to animated series. A graduate of York and TMU, Kevin has been a professor at Durham College since 2013.
