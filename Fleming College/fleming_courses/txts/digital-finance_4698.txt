Program Highlights
Fast-track your career in the financial world with this two-semester, post-graduate certificate. Gain expertise in digital finance concepts and the tools and practices required in this quickly evolving field. A combination of theory and hands-on projects, along with opportunities for industry collaboration will ensure you get the skills needed to succeed in the digital finance industry.
You will learn the fundamentals of a wide range of topics within industry, including blockchain technology, cryptocurrencies, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, data analysis, portfolio management, and risk management and mitigation.
Why Fleming?
Our School of Business and Information and Technology has long provided best-in-class programming to ensure our graduates thrive. Paired with well-connected faculty who have industry expertise, students gain opportunities for networking and mentoring to give them the head-start they need in the business world.
Experiential Learning
Apply your knowledge to a real-world challenge through the capstone applied project in semester two where you will collaborate with a community partner, employer or institution. You will gain experience in analyzing business requirements to design and recommend fintech solutions to meet strategic business priorities in industry-specific cases.
Career Opportunities
Graduates will find careers in fintech, retail banking, corporate banking, and investment agencies with roles in financial analysis, blockchain development, supply chain, auditing and investment analysis. This includes:
Business system analysis
Enterprise resource planning (ERP) analysis
Human resources information system (HRIS) analysis
Information technology systems analysis
Technical business analysis
Learning Outcomes
Analyze business requirements to inform strategic decision-making for the design and implementation of fintech solutions.
Recommend distributed ledger solutions for enterprise that enable trade using digital assets.
Evaluate and adjust policies and procedures based on legal compliance and ethical guidelines to ensure data security, integrity, and confidentiality in the delivery of fintech applications for enterprises.
Analyze interorganizational and intergovernmental structures, collaborative processes, and stakeholder relationships for transparency and accountability in decentralized governance.
Create and lead a collaborative environment to complete projects and facilitate change management in a decentralized organization context.
Design and recommend a trust framework of blockchain technology, decentralized finance and cryptocurrencies to manage records, documentation, financial transactions and controls for enterprise operations.
Design and recommend innovative technologies to create fintech solutions that meet strategic business priorities.
Design and recommend digital assets while conducting due diligence to diversify investment portfolios, optimize risk-return profiles, and meet the needs of the client.
Design and recommend digital payment systems for the financial market to meet the needs of a cashless and cardless society.
Minimum Admission Requirements
Students applying to Digital Finance must meet the following requirements:
Ontario College Diploma, Ontario College Advanced Diploma, Degree or equivalent.
Applicants who do not meet the requirements listed above who possess a combination of education and relevant experience may be considered on an individual basis.

Courses and Descriptions
Curriculum:  May 2025
Semester 1
Delivery: In Person
Blockchain Technology in Digital Finance
COMP 657
Units/ Hours: 45
Blockchain technology is an essential component of protecting assets in digital finance. Students in this course will be introduced to blockchain fundamentals, decentralized finance (DeFi), cryptocurrencies, smart contracts, and explore the role of digital finance. By engaging in case studies and group discussions, students will take steps to design and recommend blockchain solutions that meet the needs of clients and reflect regulatory and ethical standards.
Cybersecurity in Digital Finance
COMP 659
Units/ Hours: 60
With the shift to online financial services, building and maintaining cybersecurity is essential to supporting compliant and ethical business solutions. Students in this course will investigate the essential need for cybersecurity in digital finance and use case studies to explore the challenges faced by financial institutions, fintech startups, and digital finance ecosystems. By participating in group discussions, role-playing scenarios, and security-risk assessments, students will practice
strategies for safeguarding sensitive financial data to meet regulatory compliance and protect digital assets.
Data Analytics in Digital Finance
COMP 658
Units/ Hours: 60
Data analytics help inform business decisions, customer needs, and industry trends in digital finance. In this course, students will explore the fundamentals of big data, data mining techniques, predictive analytics, and machine learning algorithms to design and implement fintech solutions and technologies to meet the needs of a cashless and cardless society. Using case studies, simulations, and by engaging in group discussions, students will learn how to extract actionable insights from large datasets to improve risk management strategies, enhance financial forecasting accuracy, and optimize business performance in a digital finance ecosystem.
Fundamentals of Finance for Digital Finance
BUSN 272
Units/ Hours: 45
Digital finance, or Fintech for short, refers to the harmonization of technology and financial services in a digital environment, and this demands a strategic approach. Students in this course will explore the strategic principles of digital finance in the context of interorganizational and intergovernmental structures. Drawing on case studies, collaborative projects, and simulations, students will analyze corporate finance strategies, assets, liabilities, equity, interest rates, exchange rates, and  inflation to recommend decisions about the design of fintech solutions to
meet the needs of clients and enterprise in a digital ecosystem.
Introduction to Cryptocurrencies
BUSN 271
Units/ Hours: 60
As a form of digital transaction, cryptocurrencies are influencing trends in digital finance. Students in this course will be introduced to the origins, principles, and functionalities of cryptocurrencies and digital payment assets. By engaging with case studies, group discussions, and participating in industry specific simulations, students will explore the technical, economic, and regulatory aspects of  cryptocurrencies, and analyze the implications of these trends for financial markets, business innovation, and social transformation.
Introduction to Fintech Solutions & Digital Finance
BUSN 270
Units/ Hours: 60
Innovative technologies are transforming and shaping trends in digital finance. In this foundational course, students will be introduced to the key concepts, trends, and technologies that are moving us toward a cashless and cardless society. Using case studies, discussions, and role-playing, students will explore the ways in which blockchain technology, artificial intelligence, mobile payments, chatbots, and peer-to-peer lending are revolutionizing the traditional financial system.
Semester 2
Delivery: In Person
Advanced Topics in Digital Finance
BUSN 275
Units/ Hours: 60
Digital finance is a fast-paced industry that is constantly adapting to innovative technologies. In this course, students will be introduced to emerging and disruptive trends in digital finance by identifying the key trends and emerging players to analyze how digital transformation is driving change in the financial industry. Students will explore examples of successful fintech startups to situate disruptive business models and discuss the ways in which technologies, for example blockchain, artificial intelligence, and decentralized finance, are creating opportunities and shaping the future of digital finance to meet strategic business priorities.
Applied Fintech Project
APST 195
Units/ Hours: 60
In the applied fintech project, students will have the opportunity to solve the challenges faced by financial institutions, fintech startups, or digital finance companies. Students will analyze business requirements to design and recommend fintech solutions to meet strategic business priorities in an industry-specific context. Individual students will have the flexibility to develop and refine a workplace project in collaboration with a community partner, employer, or institution. The applied fintech project experiences and student performance will be supervised by a qualified industry professional or faculty member.
Artificial Intelligence in Digital Finance
BUSN 277
Units/ Hours: 60
Artificial intelligence has the potential to shape current and future trends in digital finance, but it is a valuable tool that can be leveraged to support risk management, legal compliance, privacy, and data security. Students in this course will explore the ways in which AI technologies can protect
digital assets, improve operational efficiency, and drive innovation in the banking industry.
Students in this course will evaluate and analyze the applications of Artificial Intelligence (AI) case studies in the banking sector, explore the ethics of AI in the context of stakeholder accountability and legal compliance, and discuss the potential and challenges of using AI to design fintech solutions.
Cryptocurrencies Portfolio Management
BUSN 276
Units/ Hours: 45
Managing cryptocurrency portfolios requires attention to the long-term objectives and risk tolerance of a client in a dynamic market. In this course, students will be introduced to the fundamental principles of portfolio management, risk assessment, asset allocation, and investment strategies. Building on what they learned in the Introduction to Cryptocurrencies course, students will engage with case studies and scenarios to navigate the dynamic and volatile nature of the cryptocurrency market, analyze investment opportunities, and construct diversified portfolios to analyze business requirements and recommend portfolio management solutions that meet the strategic needs of the client.
Fintech Startup in Digital Finance
BUSN 273
Units/ Hours: 45
Fintech startups are companies that provide financial technology solutions. Students in this course will explore the challenges and opportunities of fintech entrepreneurialism as they analyze investment opportunities, secure funding, manage financial resources, and optimize financial performance in entrepreneurial ventures. By exploring case studies of established and emerging blockchain ventures and digital payment platforms, students will analyze business requirements and collaborate with their peers to incubate a fintech startup.
Risk and Compliance in Digital Finance (REGTECH)
BUSN 274
Units/ Hours: 45
The complex regulatory landscape that governs fintech operations requires strong attention to risk mitigation to protect digital assets and meet ethical and legal compliance standards. In this course, students will explore the complexities of the regulatory landscape that governs fintech operations and analyze the policies and procedures that support data security, integrity, and confidentiality in digital finance activities. Students will analyze industry-specific case studies to design and recommend digital assets that meet compliance standards, optimize risk-return portfolios, and meet the needs of the client in digital finance environments.

Drawing on case studies, collaborative projects, and simulations, students will analyze corporate finance strategies, assets, liabilities, equity, interest rates, exchange rates, and  inflation to recommend decisions about the design of fintech solutions to
meet the needs of clients and enterprise in a digital ecosystem.
Introduction to Cryptocurrencies
BUSN 271
Units/ Hours: 60
As a form of digital transaction, cryptocurrencies are influencing trends in digital finance. Students in this course will be introduced to the origins, principles, and functionalities of cryptocurrencies and digital payment assets. By engaging with case studies, group discussions, and participating in industry specific simulations, students will explore the technical, economic, and regulatory aspects of  cryptocurrencies, and analyze the implications of these trends for financial markets, business innovation, and social transformation.
Introduction to Fintech Solutions & Digital Finance
BUSN 270
Units/ Hours: 60
Innovative technologies are transforming and shaping trends in digital finance. In this foundational course, students will be introduced to the key concepts, trends, and technologies that are moving us toward a cashless and cardless society. Using case studies, discussions, and role-playing, students will explore the ways in which blockchain technology, artificial intelligence, mobile payments, chatbots, and peer-to-peer lending are revolutionizing the traditional financial system.
Semester 2
Delivery: In Person
Advanced Topics in Digital Finance
BUSN 275
Units/ Hours: 60
Digital finance is a fast-paced industry that is constantly adapting to innovative technologies. In this course, students will be introduced to emerging and disruptive trends in digital finance by identifying the key trends and emerging players to analyze how digital transformation is driving change in the financial industry. Students will explore examples of successful fintech startups to situate disruptive business models and discuss the ways in which technologies, for example blockchain, artificial intelligence, and decentralized finance, are creating opportunities and shaping the future of digital finance to meet strategic business priorities.
Applied Fintech Project
APST 195
Units/ Hours: 60
In the applied fintech project, students will have the opportunity to solve the challenges faced by financial institutions, fintech startups, or digital finance companies. Students will analyze business requirements to design and recommend fintech solutions to meet strategic business priorities in an industry-specific context. Individual students will have the flexibility to develop and refine a workplace project in collaboration with a community partner, employer, or institution. The applied fintech project experiences and student performance will be supervised by a qualified industry professional or faculty member.
Artificial Intelligence in Digital Finance
BUSN 277
Units/ Hours: 60
Artificial intelligence has the potential to shape current and future trends in digital finance, but it is a valuable tool that can be leveraged to support risk management, legal compliance, privacy, and data security. Students in this course will explore the ways in which AI technologies can protect
digital assets, improve operational efficiency, and drive innovation in the banking industry.
Students in this course will evaluate and analyze the applications of Artificial Intelligence (AI) case studies in the banking sector, explore the ethics of AI in the context of stakeholder accountability and legal compliance, and discuss the potential and challenges of using AI to design fintech solutions.
Cryptocurrencies Portfolio Management
BUSN 276
Units/ Hours: 45
Managing cryptocurrency portfolios requires attention to the long-term objectives and risk tolerance of a client in a dynamic market. In this course, students will be introduced to the fundamental principles of portfolio management, risk assessment, asset allocation, and investment strategies. Building on what they learned in the Introduction to Cryptocurrencies course, students will engage with case studies and scenarios to navigate the dynamic and volatile nature of the cryptocurrency market, analyze investment opportunities, and construct diversified portfolios to analyze business requirements and recommend portfolio management solutions that meet the strategic needs of the client.
Fintech Startup in Digital Finance
BUSN 273
Units/ Hours: 45
Fintech startups are companies that provide financial technology solutions. Students in this course will explore the challenges and opportunities of fintech entrepreneurialism as they analyze investment opportunities, secure funding, manage financial resources, and optimize financial performance in entrepreneurial ventures. By exploring case studies of established and emerging blockchain ventures and digital payment platforms, students will analyze business requirements and collaborate with their peers to incubate a fintech startup.
Risk and Compliance in Digital Finance (REGTECH)
BUSN 274
Units/ Hours: 45
The complex regulatory landscape that governs fintech operations requires strong attention to risk mitigation to protect digital assets and meet ethical and legal compliance standards. In this course, students will explore the complexities of the regulatory landscape that governs fintech operations and analyze the policies and procedures that support data security, integrity, and confidentiality in digital finance activities. Students will analyze industry-specific case studies to design and recommend digital assets that meet compliance standards, optimize risk-return portfolios, and meet the needs of the client in digital finance environments.

Blockchain Technology in Digital Finance
COMP 657
Units/ Hours: 45

Students in this course will be introduced to blockchain fundamentals, decentralized finance (DeFi), cryptocurrencies, smart contracts, and explore the role of digital finance. Cybersecurity in Digital Finance
COMP 659
Units/ Hours: 60
With the shift to online financial services, building and maintaining cybersecurity is essential to supporting compliant and ethical business solutions. Students in this course will investigate the essential need for cybersecurity in digital finance and use case studies to explore the challenges faced by financial institutions, fintech startups, and digital finance ecosystems. By participating in group discussions, role-playing scenarios, and security-risk assessments, students will practice
strategies for safeguarding sensitive financial data to meet regulatory compliance and protect digital assets.

COMP 658
Units/ Hours: 60

In this course, students will explore the fundamentals of big data, data mining techniques, predictive analytics, and machine learning algorithms to design and implement fintech solutions and technologies to meet the needs of a cashless and cardless society. Using case studies, simulations, and by engaging in group discussions, students will learn how to extract actionable insights from large datasets to improve risk management strategies, enhance financial forecasting accuracy, and optimize business performance in a digital finance ecosystem.
