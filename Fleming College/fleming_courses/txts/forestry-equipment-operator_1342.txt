Program Highlights
This 15-week certificate program provides you with introductory training on heavy forestry equipment as well as education in forestry principles and proper safety techniques. You will get hands-on experience operating machinery and performing preventative maintenance and minor repairs. The program also includes the basics of forestry operations, harvest systems, and forest fire preparedness.
Why Choose Fleming
Fleming has been a leader in Forestry education for over 55 years – we have the largest college Forestry program in Canada. We are also a renowned training hub for heavy equipment technology. Combining these two specialties, we can offer this unique program within our exceptional learning environments. You will have access to a range of industry equipment right on-campus as well as state-of-the-art simulators to enhance your real-world training.
Career Opportunities
Demand for employees in the Forestry sector is growing. Careers for graduates within this program can include:
Logging and machinery operators
Silviculture and forestry workers
Chainsaw and skidder operators
Logging and forestry labourers
Heavy equipment operators
Minimum Admission Requirements
Students applying to Forestry Equipment Operator must meet the following requirements:
OSSD with majority of credits at the Workplace (E) level including Gr 12 (E) English
Where Workplace level courses are listed, C, U and M courses will be accepted.
For programs requiring a majority of credits at the Workplace level, OLC4O will be counted as an acceptable English course.
Mature Students
If you are 19 years of age or older before classes start, and you do not possess an OSSD, you can write the Skillsmarker to assess your
eligibility for admission
. Additional testing or academic upgrading may be necessary to meet specific course requirements for this program.
Learning Outcomes
Identify, wear, and maintain appropriate personal protective equipment in accordance with legislation, safety standards, and industry practices.
Perform safety inspections and preventative maintenance for forest harvesting machines according to legislation, industry standards, and manufacturer specifications.
Explain silvicultural systems, and operations in implementation of forest management operations.
Identify, measure, and assess trees using common forestry tools and resources for use in implementing silvicultural operations.
Operate forestry machinery to harvest timber safely and effectively.
Safely operate and maintain small equipment used in timber harvesting and processing.

Courses and Descriptions
Curriculum:  September 2025
Semester 1
Delivery: In Person
Forest Equipment Operator Field Camp
APST 184
Units/ Hours: 160
Forestry workers require in-field experience with silviculture tools, as well as small and heavy equipment. In this field camp, students practice using forest inventory tools, perform tree identification and inspecting harvest compliance.  Students gain experience using small, powered equipment such as water pumps for fire suppression as well as chainsaws including to complete the Workplace Safety North Introduction to Professional Chainsaw Operation Common Core Course. Students also gain experience for safe and competently use of heavy forest harvesting equipment, maintaining a safe worksite, and performing routine field maintenance and inspections.
Forest Equipment Operator Simulators
FSTY 113
Units/ Hours: 80
To introduce the controls, handling and actions of forestry equipment, students gain experience on full-scale simulator operator stations. Using progressive learning modules, students learn how to operate and position forestry equipment including harvester and forwarder.
Forest Industry Wildland Firefighting
FSTY 111
Units/ Hours: 20
On Ontario's Crown Lands, forest workers must be trained and capable to take part in fire suppression under the Modifying Industrial Operations Protocol. Following the SP 102 Forest Firefighter Training Manual, students learn about health and safety, fire terminology and behaviour, and the basics of fire suppression.
Harvest Safety and Site Management
FSTY 108
Units/ Hours: 30
Worker and worksite safety is critical in forestry operations. This course introduces students to key safety considerations required for Forestry Equipment Operators including legislation and personal protective measures. Students will be introduced to typical forest harvest operation site management as well as common hazards and methods to reduce risks and prevent injury.
Introduction to Forestry
FSTY 109
Units/ Hours: 30
Forest equipment operators that understand the foundations of forestry improve the success of meeting silvicultural objectives. This course provides a broad introduction to the forestry industry, forest management and silviculture with a focus on timber harvesting. Students learn about the basics of silvicultural systems, tree marking, harvest block layout and harvest methods as well as common Ontario tree species identification, forest inventory measurements and ecological and cultural conservation concerns.
Introduction to Operation, Maintenance and Safety
FSTY 114
Units/ Hours: 80
Safety and maintenance are critical aspects of operating heavy equipment.  Students will learn about safety regulations, machinery components, and mechanics as well as perform equipment inspection procedures, typical recurring maintenance tasks, and minor repairs. Students also begin in-field operations to learn the fundamentals of heavy equipment operation.
WSN Common Core Courses
FSTY 110
Units/ Hours: 8
Forest Equipment Operators are required to complete Workplace Safety North's Mechanical Harvesting/Forestry Pit and Road Construction Equipment Operator Common Core (4-6 hour external online course). This course introduces the content that needs to be successfully completed by all students.
