Program Highlights
Join the booming beauty and wellness industry with this well-rounded, fast-track program. You will learn how to provide a range of treatments, including :
Manicures,
Pedicures,
Skincare,
Body treatments, and
Waxing techniques and
Introductory Medical Esthetics, Oncology Esthetics and Holistic Wellness Esthetics.
You will also gain skills in Spa Management that include:
Marketing and Social Media
Retail sales
Spa Planning
Entrepreneurship
We have designed the program to include:
Aromatherapy: Learn the art and science of Essential Oils using our award-winning, ethically produced European Aromatherapy skin care line.
Three Advanced professional products lines: Aromatherapy, Medical and Acne.
Only Holistic and vegan products for hand and foot care .
Choose Fleming
Fleming’s Esthetican and Spa Management program has an excellent reputation in the beauty and wellness industry. Students choose this program because it offers:
A fast-track to your career: 3 consecutive semesters with start dates every semester
Small class sizes
On-campus spa for hands-on learning
About 200 hours of experiential learning with clients
Field to trips that include Ste. Anne’s Spa and The Grail Springs Spa
Hydrotherapy training session at Ste. Anne's Spa
Our state-of-the-art learning labs set us apart. These include:
Manicure/Pedicure Lab:
6 Pedicure thrones
12 Manicure tables
Hot towel cabinets
Full line of LCN nail polish
Full line of Magic Gel gel polish
Facial and Body Treatment Lab:
Your own sink, hot towel cabinet, fully stocked linens and products at each station
Privacy curtains
Retail display area to practice sales skills
A variety of facial machines and equipment, including:
Microdermabrasion
High frequency
Galvanic
Steamers
Hydrobrasion (Medical)
OxyGeneo (Medical)
LED lights
Work Experience
You will be well-prepared and in demand when you graduate.
Your entire placement takes place in our state-of-the-art spa.
You will have about 200 hours of experiential learning with clients in Semester 2 and 3
Integrated into your experience are spa management retail sales skills to boost your earning potential
Is this You?
Success in this career requires these skills and traits:
Passion for beauty and wellness
Strong communication and interpersonal skills
Team player
Conscientious
Compassionate and caring

Indigenous Perspectives Designation
The Indigenous Perspectives Designation (IPD) is an option available to students in this program. To qualify for an IPD, you must take and successfully complete a series of required Indigenous Studies courses as well as participate in a minimum of four approved co-curricular Indigenous events or experiences. Your transcript will indicate the IPD upon graduation, a designation that provides you with a comprehensive understanding of Indigenous cultures, histories, traditions, and contributions to our shared society within the context of your program of study. The IPD provides a valuable skill set sought after in today's employment sector.
Learning Outcomes
Perform a variety of specialized body and skin care treatments following correct procedures and precautions and supporting client needs (including and not limited to facials, manicures, pedicures, hair removal, and make up applications).
use a range of specialized equipment and products, in compliance with established national, provincial, industry, and other related standards, regulations, policies, and procedures.
Apply relevant knowledge of anatomy, physiology, and histology to the provision of specialized esthetic treatments and services.
Adhere to health, safety, sanitation, and infection and prevention control guidelines, according to current legislation and national, provincial, municipal, and industry standards and regulations.
Identify business skills and activities required for the successful establishment and operation of a small esthetic business in a salon or spa environment.
Select and recommend the use of esthetic products and product ingredients to clients, taking into account health status and identified needs.
Establish and maintain professional relationships in adherence to standards and ethics associated with the profession.
Develop customer service strategies that meet and adapt to individual needs and expectations in accordance with professional standards and ethics.
Determine professional development strategies that lead to the enhancement of work performance and career opportunities and keep pace with industry change.
Create a comprehensive business plan to open or manage a spa operation that adheres to professional standards and practices
Adapt marketing strategies for esthetics small business using marketing concepts and technologies that suit the evolving environment and client needs
Minimum Admission Requirements
Students applying to Esthetician – Spa Management must meet the following requirements:
OSSD with majority of credits at the Workplace (E) level including Gr 12 (E)English
Where Workplace level courses are listed, C, U and M courses will be accepted.
For programs requiring a majority of credits at the Workplace level, OLC4O will be counted as an acceptable English course.
Mature Students
If you are 19 years of age or older before classes start, and you do not possess an OSSD, you can write the
Skillsmarker
to assess your eligibility for admission. Additional testing or academic upgrading may be necessary to meet specific course requirements for this program.

Courses and Descriptions
Curriculum:  January 2025
Semester 1
Delivery: In Person
Communications I for Health Professionals
COMM 252
Units/ Hours: 45
Communications 1 for Health Professionals is an introductory course that provides a foundation in college-level communications relevant to professions in health. In this course, students learn to read critically, write appropriately for a variety of audiences, conduct research, cite references, revise for clarity and correctness, and present those results in various formats. In seminars, labs, and modules students will engage in both independent and collaborative activities relevant to health studies, including the development and presentation of a research assignment to help them become more effective communicators in academic and professional environments within in the field of health.
Fundamentals of Skin Care
COSM  47
Units/ Hours: 75
Skin care and facials are a cornerstone of the esthetics industry allowing the esthetician to support clients with their skin and wellness needs. This course introduces the student to skin histology and physiology, to gain a foundational level of knowledge required to perform basic and advanced manual facial techniques. Students will learn how to perform in-depth consultation, with an awareness of contra-indications and the importance of client care. Through hands on learning, theoretical knowledge will be transferred into the practical labs for students to carry out full skin analysis to determine skin type, conditions, being able to distinguish between skin disorders and diseases, to safely perform a variety of facials to accommodate client's needs. Makeup techniques and current trends will be introduced to assist students to build their skills at applying makeup in a professional manner. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Co-Requisites
Introduction to Aromatherapy and Cosmetic Chemistry (COSM  48)
Introduction to Aromatherapy and Cosmetic Chemistry
COSM  48
Units/ Hours: 75
Aromatherapy is an integral part of facial and body treatments that incorporate essential oils to support scientific ingredients in skin care products, which promotes a client's psychological and physical well-being. This course will introduce the students to foundations of the art and science of aromatherapy, combined with an overview of the basics of cosmetic chemistry and makeup formulations. The course will explore the ethics of sustainable ingredient harvesting and the production of safe, non- harmful cosmetic products. The student will build on their skin physiology and product knowledge, to determine product choice and home-care recommendations during the practical facial lab. Further advanced techniques of facial lymphatic drainage enhanced with essential oils will be taught and incorporated into all facials. Opportunity will be facilitated to allow students to practice the art of blending essential oils to create their own products for use in the practical lab. Makeup techniques and current trends will be introduced to assist students to build their skills at applying in a professional manner. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Co-Requisites
Fundamentals of Skin Care (COSM  47)
Manicure
COSM   3
Units/ Hours: 45
Manicures are one of the most requested services in the Spa industry. Students will learn wet and dry manicures which are a fundamental skill for success in esthetics. Manicure skills of natural nail filing, cuticle work, hand massage, professional polish application, and decontamination will be learned and practiced. All aspects of nail disorders, related diseases and contra-indications are discussed. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Co-Requisites
Pedicure (COSM   6)
Pedicure
COSM   6
Units/ Hours: 75
Pedicures are not only a luxury service, for some they are a necessity for people who can no longer care for their own feet. You will learn and practice the latest pedicure techniques including toenail trimming, callous removal, cuticle work, foot massage, and professional polish application. Nail disorders, related disease and contra-indications are a focal point of this course. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Co-Requisites
Manicure (COSM   3)
Spa Sales and Marketing
BUSN 259
Units/ Hours: 30
Self promotion, marketing and retail sales in the spa industry is key to having a successful business. Students will create their own ethical and professional Social Media accounts using different platforms to self promote their business model and mission statement, linking to services, products and online booking and sales. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Introduction to Indigenous Studies
INDG  49
Units/ Hours: 45
This course is an introduction to the study of Indigenous (First Nations, Metis, and Inuit) peoples in Canada. Students will explore the complex historical and contemporary relationships between Indigenous and non-Indigenous peoples. The course will also guide students to begin to understand the diversity and depth of Indigenous societies, worldviews, and knowledge through a multi-disciplinary lens.

SCIE 192
Units/ Hours: 30
This biology course is specifically designed with the esthetician in mind. Topics will include cellular knowledge in relation to cosmetic chemistry and wound healing in medical esthetics treatments. Workbooks and assignments will ensure students have gained the knowledge required by the spa industry to meet client needs.

require general education credits
. These courses allow you to explore issues of societal concern by looking at the history, theory and contemporary applications of those issues.
Your program has designated some required general education courses. In addition, you have the opportunity to
choose from a list of electives
each semester.
Many of these courses and some other general education courses are also available through evening classes, by distance education, or on-line. See our Part-Time Studies Calendar for these opportunities.
You may already possess general education equivalencies from other colleges or universities. Please see the General Education Co-ordinators at the Peterborough and Lindsay locations for possible exemptions.
In order to obtain the IPD, students must choose INDG128 as their Gen Ed Elective in either semester 2 or 3.

Semester 1
Delivery: In Person
Communications I for Health Professionals
COMM 252
Units/ Hours: 45
Communications 1 for Health Professionals is an introductory course that provides a foundation in college-level communications relevant to professions in health. In this course, students learn to read critically, write appropriately for a variety of audiences, conduct research, cite references, revise for clarity and correctness, and present those results in various formats. In seminars, labs, and modules students will engage in both independent and collaborative activities relevant to health studies, including the development and presentation of a research assignment to help them become more effective communicators in academic and professional environments within in the field of health.
Fundamentals of Skin Care
COSM  47
Units/ Hours: 75
Skin care and facials are a cornerstone of the esthetics industry allowing the esthetician to support clients with their skin and wellness needs. This course introduces the student to skin histology and physiology, to gain a foundational level of knowledge required to perform basic and advanced manual facial techniques. Students will learn how to perform in-depth consultation, with an awareness of contra-indications and the importance of client care. Through hands on learning, theoretical knowledge will be transferred into the practical labs for students to carry out full skin analysis to determine skin type, conditions, being able to distinguish between skin disorders and diseases, to safely perform a variety of facials to accommodate client's needs. Makeup techniques and current trends will be introduced to assist students to build their skills at applying makeup in a professional manner. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Co-Requisites
Introduction to Aromatherapy and Cosmetic Chemistry (COSM  48)
Introduction to Aromatherapy and Cosmetic Chemistry
COSM  48
Units/ Hours: 75
Aromatherapy is an integral part of facial and body treatments that incorporate essential oils to support scientific ingredients in skin care products, which promotes a client's psychological and physical well-being. This course will introduce the students to foundations of the art and science of aromatherapy, combined with an overview of the basics of cosmetic chemistry and makeup formulations. The course will explore the ethics of sustainable ingredient harvesting and the production of safe, non- harmful cosmetic products. The student will build on their skin physiology and product knowledge, to determine product choice and home-care recommendations during the practical facial lab. Further advanced techniques of facial lymphatic drainage enhanced with essential oils will be taught and incorporated into all facials. Opportunity will be facilitated to allow students to practice the art of blending essential oils to create their own products for use in the practical lab. Makeup techniques and current trends will be introduced to assist students to build their skills at applying in a professional manner. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Co-Requisites
Fundamentals of Skin Care (COSM  47)
Manicure
COSM   3
Units/ Hours: 45
Manicures are one of the most requested services in the Spa industry. Students will learn wet and dry manicures which are a fundamental skill for success in esthetics. Manicure skills of natural nail filing, cuticle work, hand massage, professional polish application, and decontamination will be learned and practiced. All aspects of nail disorders, related diseases and contra-indications are discussed. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Co-Requisites
Pedicure (COSM   6)
Pedicure
COSM   6
Units/ Hours: 75
Pedicures are not only a luxury service, for some they are a necessity for people who can no longer care for their own feet. You will learn and practice the latest pedicure techniques including toenail trimming, callous removal, cuticle work, foot massage, and professional polish application. Nail disorders, related disease and contra-indications are a focal point of this course. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Co-Requisites
Manicure (COSM   3)
Spa Sales and Marketing
BUSN 259
Units/ Hours: 30
Self promotion, marketing and retail sales in the spa industry is key to having a successful business. Students will create their own ethical and professional Social Media accounts using different platforms to self promote their business model and mission statement, linking to services, products and online booking and sales. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Introduction to Indigenous Studies
INDG  49
Units/ Hours: 45
This course is an introduction to the study of Indigenous (First Nations, Metis, and Inuit) peoples in Canada. Students will explore the complex historical and contemporary relationships between Indigenous and non-Indigenous peoples. The course will also guide students to begin to understand the diversity and depth of Indigenous societies, worldviews, and knowledge through a multi-disciplinary lens.

Semester 1

Communications I for Health Professionals
COMM 252
Units/ Hours: 45
Communications 1 for Health Professionals is an introductory course that provides a foundation in college-level communications relevant to professions in health. In this course, students learn to read critically, write appropriately for a variety of audiences, conduct research, cite references, revise for clarity and correctness, and present those results in various formats. In seminars, labs, and modules students will engage in both independent and collaborative activities relevant to health studies, including the development and presentation of a research assignment to help them become more effective communicators in academic and professional environments within in the field of health.
Fundamentals of Skin Care
COSM  47
Units/ Hours: 75
Skin care and facials are a cornerstone of the esthetics industry allowing the esthetician to support clients with their skin and wellness needs. This course introduces the student to skin histology and physiology, to gain a foundational level of knowledge required to perform basic and advanced manual facial techniques. Students will learn how to perform in-depth consultation, with an awareness of contra-indications and the importance of client care. Through hands on learning, theoretical knowledge will be transferred into the practical labs for students to carry out full skin analysis to determine skin type, conditions, being able to distinguish between skin disorders and diseases, to safely perform a variety of facials to accommodate client's needs. Makeup techniques and current trends will be introduced to assist students to build their skills at applying makeup in a professional manner. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Co-Requisites
Introduction to Aromatherapy and Cosmetic Chemistry (COSM  48)
Introduction to Aromatherapy and Cosmetic Chemistry
COSM  48
Units/ Hours: 75
Aromatherapy is an integral part of facial and body treatments that incorporate essential oils to support scientific ingredients in skin care products, which promotes a client's psychological and physical well-being. This course will introduce the students to foundations of the art and science of aromatherapy, combined with an overview of the basics of cosmetic chemistry and makeup formulations. The course will explore the ethics of sustainable ingredient harvesting and the production of safe, non- harmful cosmetic products. The student will build on their skin physiology and product knowledge, to determine product choice and home-care recommendations during the practical facial lab. Further advanced techniques of facial lymphatic drainage enhanced with essential oils will be taught and incorporated into all facials. Opportunity will be facilitated to allow students to practice the art of blending essential oils to create their own products for use in the practical lab. Makeup techniques and current trends will be introduced to assist students to build their skills at applying in a professional manner. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Co-Requisites
Fundamentals of Skin Care (COSM  47)
Manicure
COSM   3
Units/ Hours: 45
Manicures are one of the most requested services in the Spa industry. Students will learn wet and dry manicures which are a fundamental skill for success in esthetics. Manicure skills of natural nail filing, cuticle work, hand massage, professional polish application, and decontamination will be learned and practiced. All aspects of nail disorders, related diseases and contra-indications are discussed. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Co-Requisites
Pedicure (COSM   6)
Pedicure
COSM   6
Units/ Hours: 75
Pedicures are not only a luxury service, for some they are a necessity for people who can no longer care for their own feet. You will learn and practice the latest pedicure techniques including toenail trimming, callous removal, cuticle work, foot massage, and professional polish application. Nail disorders, related disease and contra-indications are a focal point of this course. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Co-Requisites
Manicure (COSM   3)
Spa Sales and Marketing
BUSN 259
Units/ Hours: 30
Self promotion, marketing and retail sales in the spa industry is key to having a successful business. Students will create their own ethical and professional Social Media accounts using different platforms to self promote their business model and mission statement, linking to services, products and online booking and sales. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Introduction to Indigenous Studies
INDG  49
Units/ Hours: 45
This course is an introduction to the study of Indigenous (First Nations, Metis, and Inuit) peoples in Canada. Students will explore the complex historical and contemporary relationships between Indigenous and non-Indigenous peoples. The course will also guide students to begin to understand the diversity and depth of Indigenous societies, worldviews, and knowledge through a multi-disciplinary lens.

Advanced Hand and Foot Treatments
COSM  49
Units/ Hours: 45
Hand and Foot Treatments are common services in the Spa industry. Students will learn hot stone massage, exfoliants and masks, nail art, gel polish application and removal. Utilizing specialized machines and products, Medical Pedicuring for clients with fungal infections or diabetes will be learned. Decontamination is reinforced, and nail disorders and related diseases are further explored. Students will demonstrate their skills through practical assessments.

Biology and Nutrition for Esthetics
SCIE 192
Units/ Hours: 30
This biology course is specifically designed with the esthetician in mind. Topics will include cellular knowledge in relation to cosmetic chemistry and wound healing in medical esthetics treatments. Wellness for human biological and physiological systems to achieve homeostasis is discussed. Fundamentals of nutrition for the health of skin and nails is introduced. Workbooks and assignments will ensure students have gained the knowledge required by the spa industry to meet client needs.

Hair Removal
COSM   7
Units/ Hours: 45
Hair removal, lash and brow services are some of the most popular requested services in the spa industry. Students will become proficient in a variety of hair removal techniques and treatments including hard and soft wax techniques, eyebrow sculpting, and full body hair removal. Introduction to Oncology Esthetics
COSM  42
Units/ Hours: 30
Many people will be affected by cancer in their lifetime. This course will teach a fundamental understanding of treating an oncology client ethically, safely, and compassionately within the esthetics scope of practice. Spa Planning and Entrepreneurship
BUSN 260
Units/ Hours: 30
Sole-proprietorship is a common business model in the esthetics industry. Students will learn the importance of financial planning, facilities, inventory management, employment law, and insurance considerations. This course will teach the basics of day-to-day spa operations including sensible and ethical decision making. Assessments include a full business plan that can be used to apply for funds to turn your goals in to a reality.
