Program Highlights
Create compelling digital customer experiences with this two-semester graduate certificate. You will learn about various digital tools and technologies as well as learn to create project management frameworks, digital strategies and tactics. Combine theory and hands-on learning to gain the skills needed to adapt in a constantly evolving digital marketing landscape.
As part of the program, you will learn to identify and articulate customer segments, assess the fundamentals of effective search engine optimization (SEO) and search engine marketing (SEM) campaigns, gain hands-on experience in planning and implementing real-world digital marketing campaigns, and discover how data analytics plays a pivotal role in shaping marketing decisions.
Why Choose Fleming?
Our suite of foundational Business and Information Technology programs have led to countless successful careers for our graduates. The experienced and dedicated faculty will ensure you get the most relevant knowledge and skills, and their connections to industry allow for valuable networking and mentoring opportunities.
Experiential Learning
In the last semester of the program, you will have a chance to put all the knowledge you have developed throughout the program toward an applied project. Collaborate with a real-world client to develop and execute a comprehensive digital marketing plan. The applied project is a great opportunity for you to showcase your expertise, creativity, and decision-making skills while making a meaningful impact in the digital marketing landscape.
Career Opportunities
The field of digital marketing is experiencing substantial growth in Canada, which is projected to continue over the long term. Careers could include:
Digital marketing and communications
Marketing project management
Advertising accounts management
Media coordination

Learning Outcomes
Create strategic digital marketing plans with elements such as paid advertising, organic content, engagement tactics, and performance metrics to achieve a client's objective(s).
Use digital marketing tools and technologies to execute campaigns, automate processes, and enhance marketing efficiency and effectiveness.
Propose creative strategies for a digital landscape to achieve marketing goals and objectives.
Construct digital narratives and personalized experiences for target audiences across digital platforms and channels to support digital engagement strategies and optimize user experience.
Formulate data-driven digital marketing strategies that align with business goals, consumer needs and market trends.
Complete all work in compliance with legal and ethical digital marketing practices.
Use project management principles and tools to complete collaborative digital marketing campaigns on time and on budget.
Leverage AI technologies to stay current and competitive in the field of digital marketing.
Minimum Admission Requirements
Students applying to Digital Marketing must meet the following requirements:
Ontario College Diploma, Ontario College Advanced Diploma, Degree, or equivalent
Applicants who do not meet the requirements listed above who possess a combination of education and relevant experience may be considered on an individual basis.

Courses and Descriptions
Curriculum:  January 2025
Semester 1
Delivery: In Person
Artificial Intelligence: Digital Marketing Supports
BUSN 278
Units/ Hours: 45
Learn to lead in the evolving digital landscape with innovative AI-powered marketing solutions. Explore how AI technologies are revolutionizing digital marketing practices, from predictive analytics to personalized targeting. Through practical, hands-on activities, students gain experience in leveraging AI tools to optimize work systems and craft dynamic digital marketing strategies that resonate with audiences and support marketing activities, while complying with legal and ethical practice when leveraging appropriate AI tools.
Crafting a Digital Narrative
BUSN 279
Units/ Hours: 45
Unleash your creativity as you learn the art of storytelling in the digital age. Delve into the principles of narrative construction, visual storytelling and design, and audience engagement across various digital platforms and channels. Through practical, hands-on activities, you will practice crafting compelling digital narratives that ethically captivate audiences and leave a lasting impression in an evolving and competitive digital landscape.
Digital Marketing Strategy
MKTG 168
Units/ Hours: 45
Learn to integrate content creation, engagement tactics and performance metrics into strategic digital marketing plans to achieve objectives. You will explore data-driven approaches to align business goals with consumer needs and market trends, while critically evaluating the balance between paid advertising and organic content strategies.
Introduction to Digital Marketing
MKTG 166
Units/ Hours: 45
A comprehensive introduction to the fundamental concepts and strategies of digital marketing in a professional environment sets students up for success in the program and their career. Topics include common digital tools, technologies, plans, project management frameworks, digital strategies and tactics. Combining theory and practical activities, students will gain the necessary skills to navigate the ever-evolving landscape of digital marketing and succeed in today's competitive marketplace.
Strategic Social Media
MKTG 167
Units/ Hours: 45
As a digital marketer, you need to entice your audience to stop scrolling through their social feed and engage with your content. Through a variety of activities, you will learn to captivate audiences and drive meaningful engagement by identifying appropriate target audiences, selecting the right platforms, developing compelling social strategies, and creating content that resonates across diverse social channels and complies with privacy requirements and platform rules.
Tools of the Digital Marketing Trade I
MKTG 169
Units/ Hours: 60
There are many tools available that support different facets of digital  marketing. Which ones do you choose, when, and why? Through a variety of activities, students will gain practical skills in graphic design, website development, prototyping, and marketing automation while learning to navigate digital marketing tools with confidence.
What's in a Website?
COMP 660
Units/ Hours: 45
Dive into the anatomy of a website, exploring design elements, usability principles, and conversion optimization techniques. Gain practical skills in evaluating website performance, from user experience to functionality, and learn how to optimize for engagement and conversion. Through course activities, you will dissect successful website strategies and apply best practices to enhance owned digital media. Whether you are a marketer, designer, or entrepreneur, this course equips you with the tools and knowledge to create compelling websites that captivate audiences and drive business growth.
Semester 2
Delivery: In Person
Balancing Paid and Organic Content
MKTG 172
Units/ Hours: 45
With the increase of ad-blockers and continual scrolling, marketers must understand the intricacies of leveraging both paid advertising and organic strategies to maximize reach, engagement, and conversion rates. Explore how to design integrated digital marketing campaigns that combine the strengths of paid promotion with the authenticity of organic content. Through a mix of theory, case studies, and practical activities, you will evaluate the effectiveness of different approaches and optimize your content mix to support marketing goals.
Consumer Behaviour and Experience Design
MKTG 171
Units/ Hours: 45
Step into the minds of consumers and master the art of user experience design as you explore techniques for analysing user data, identifying patterns, and designing personalized experiences that enhance engagement and loyalty. Through practical exercises, learn to drive meaningful interactions and achieve marketing objectives through  rototyping and interpreting observational market research.
Digital Leads: Captivate, Capture and Convert
BUSN 281
Units/ Hours: 45
Have you ever considered why you feel compelled to download a free resource or trade your email address for a promo code? Digital Marketing Capstone (Applied Learning)
APST 196
Units/ Hours: 30
Put all the skills you have developed throughout the program to work as you  collaborate with a real-world client to develop and execute a comprehensive digital marketing plan. This culminating experience allows you to showcase your expertise, creativity, and ethical decision-making while making a meaningful impact in the digital marketing landscape. Prepare to emerge from this course, capable of collaboratively managing a project and being ready to launch successful digital marketing campaigns and excel in your future career endeavours.
Digital Marketing Case Studies
MKTG 170
Units/ Hours: 45
Learn about what goes into the decisions that result in successful and unsuccessful digital marketing campaigns, including elements of ethical practice, creative storytelling, and memorable reactions. Through interactive discussions and hands-on exercises, you will gain invaluable insights into the nuances of digital marketing tactics, from SEO and content marketing to social media and email campaigns through digital marketing case studies and how to apply key learnings to your own marketing endeavours.
Insights and Analytics
BUSN 280
Units/ Hours: 45
Interpreting data is a key skill digital marketers need to be strategic and successful in a fast-paced, changing environment. Through case studies and analysis, students will gain proficiency in interpreting data, identifying trends, and making informed decisions to drive marketing strategy that align with business objectives and consumer needs.
Tools of the Digital Marketing Trade II
MKTG 173
