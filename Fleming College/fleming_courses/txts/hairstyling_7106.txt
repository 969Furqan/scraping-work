Program Highlights
This compressed program, designed by award-winning industry professionals, combines extensive hands-on training and theory to prepare you for a career as licensed professional hairstylist.
You will learn and practice the latest skills in hairstyling techniques, including cutting, styling, naturally derived smoothing treatment, colouring, hair extensions and multicultural hairstyling in the newly designed on-campus salon within Fleming’s Spa + Clinic multidisciplinary environment.
In Fleming’s newly designed onsite Academy Salon you will use environmentally sustainable products and practices to support health, ecological, social and economic wellbeing. The tools in your kit have been meticulously sourced to ensure you are well-equipped from the start.
Community involvement is an important cornerstone within our School. In the final semester you will take part in community outreach days, practicing your skills and building relationships with marginalized groups. Students in the graduating semester also organize and deliver a unique hair show with local community partners. This program also offers the opportunity to gain the Indigenous Perspectives Designation.
Why choose Fleming?
Hairstyling at Fleming offers an exceptional combination of features that set it apart from similar programs:
Extensive hands-on practice in a newly designed on-campus Academy Salon
A curriculum that embodies the pillars of hairstyling: artistic skills, science, and social confidence
An emphasis on environmentally sustainable products and practices
Entrepreneurial and business skills to support salon ownership
Multicultural hairstyling techniques
A faculty of award-winning, practicing professionals
Indigenous Perspectives Designation integrated into the curriculum
A kit of high-quality tools, which you will be able to use in your career, is included in your tuition. The kit is valued at approximately $1,000.
Practical Experience
The program includes two unique 120-hour experiential learning practicums: Academy Salon 1 in semester two, and Academy Salon 2 in semester three.
During the first practicum, you will work on clients and participate in creating an exceptional salon experience, from the initial greeting, consultation, preparation, service, and client haircare.
In the second practicum, you will continue to develop professional skills in shape, colour, and session styling. You will also be fully immersed in all daily business activities, with a focus on client rapport building, retention, retail expertise, and overall exceptional client experiences. The Academy Salon will provide a platform to build your soft skills to become a well-rounded professional.
Is this for you?
Whether you are a high school graduate, mature student or someone looking for a career change in a creative field, this program can offer you an exceptional entry into the hairstyling business.
With an emphasis on entrepreneurship and hair salon business skills that include client rapport and retention, retail experience and essential business soft skills, you will be well-prepared to become your own boss or offer your future employer well-rounded skills that go beyond hairstyling skills.

Learning Outcomes
Complete all work in adherence to professional ethics, government regulations, workplace standards and policies, and according to manufacturer's specifications as applicable.
Facilitate the provision of healthy and safe working environments and perform sanitization procedures in accordance with related health regulations and legislation
Apply entrepreneurial skills to the operation and administration of a hair stylist business.
Adapt to various and changing technologies, applications and procedures in the hair styling industry, and develop and present a plan outlining future professional development.
Develop and use client service strategies that meet and adapt to individual client needs and expectations.
Select and administer preparatory procedures and/or treatments to the hair and scalp using individually selected products to meet the expectations of the client
Identify, select and use a variety of tools to cut hair according to the needs and expectations of the client.
Select and use standard and specialized techniques to effectively style wet and dry hair.
Perform a permanent wave using current and relevant methods according to hair type and style.
Chemically relax hair by selecting and applying relevant knowledge of, and skills with, chemical products and techniques in order to meet the needs and expectation of the client.
Colour, lighten, tone, highlight and lowlight hair, and/or remove pigment to the level of colour desired.
Compare and contrast fibre types, application procedures, specialized tools, and maintenance procedures when working with hair additions.
Perform hairstyling using environmentally sustainable practices and products to support health, ecological, social, and economic well-being
Minimum Admission Requirements
Students applying to Hairstyling must meet the following requirements:
OSSD with majority of credits at the Workplace (E) level including Gr 12 (E)English
Where Workplace level courses are listed, C, U and M courses will be accepted.
For programs requiring a majority of credits at the Workplace level, OLC4O will be counted as an acceptable English course.

Courses and Descriptions
Curriculum:  January 2025
Semester 1
Delivery: Hybrid
Communications I for Health Professionals
COMM 252
Units/ Hours: 45
Communications 1 for Health Professionals is an introductory course that provides a foundation in college-level communications relevant to professions in health. In this course, students learn to read critically, write appropriately for a variety of audiences, conduct research, cite references, revise for clarity and correctness, and present those results in various formats. In seminars, labs, and modules students will engage in both independent and collaborative activities relevant to health studies, including the development and presentation of a research assignment to help them become more effective communicators in academic and professional environments within in the field of health.
Foundations of Colour I
HSTL   3
Units/ Hours: 105
As a colour artist, the hair is our canvas.  Understanding the theory of the colour wheel and its relationship to colour formulation and application techniques is pivotal to becoming an outstanding colourist. An in-depth colour consultation system will be taught to establish the client's natural hair colour level to determine the achievability of the desired outcome. The theory of colour formulation will assist students to build confidence in achieving consistent results in lifting or depositing colour through a variety of techniques to colour hair using temporary, semi-permanent, demi-permanent, or permanent colour. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Foundations of Session Styling I
HSTL   2
Units/ Hours: 75
The craft of hairdressing goes beyond the salon, where scissors are rarely required. This is the world of editorial, film, fashion, bridal, and avant-garde. In this course, students will study the different genres of dressing hair, placements, braiding, techniques, tools, and the use of various pins and supportive products.  
Learn the famous beach waves, runway ponytail, classic chignons and how to begin branding your signature as a finisher. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Foundations of Shape I
HSTL   1
Units/ Hours: 135
Shaping is the cornerstone of the hairstyling industry. Each stylist: a sculptor; the medium: hair. Students will learn in-depth consultations techniques with awareness to, hair texture, lifestyle, and face shape. This course introduces the student to the art of interior and exterior shaping with the underpinning knowledge to distribution and parting hair. Students will learn foundational layering, framing, and blow-drying methods. Through hands-on learning, theoretical knowledge will be transferred into the practical labs. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Guest Artist Experience
HSTL   5
Units/ Hours: 45
This course introduces students to the hairstyling industry from both guest and artist perspectives, emphasizing the importance of effective communication and the unique journey of hairstylists. Students will explore the techniques and stories of industry experts, gaining valuable insights into the art of hairstyling and meaningful connections. Learning activities include research, role-playing, and a combination of theoretical and practical assessments.
Marketing, Branding and Social Media
BUSN 262
Units/ Hours: 30
Self-promotion, marketing, and retail sales in the spa industry is key to having a successful business. Students will create their own ethical and professional Social Media accounts using different platforms to self-promote their business model and mission statement, linking to services, products and online booking and sales. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Product Knowledge and Customer Care
HSTL   4
Units/ Hours: 30
Understanding ingredients and the formulations of products is a key component in the journey to becoming a successful hairstylist. Students will learn how the selection of professional products plays an integral role throughout shape, colour and session styling to create the hair design. Throughout this course the student will learn the importance of the following topics: client-focused approach to retailing, the importance of using hair care products that support ethical and sustainable farming practices, natural versus synthetic, and the ethics of using cruelty-free products. Knowledge will be shown through theoretical assessments.
Introduction to Indigenous Studies
INDG  49
Units/ Hours: 45
This course is an introduction to the study of Indigenous (First Nations, Metis, and Inuit) peoples in Canada. Students will explore the complex historical and contemporary relationships between Indigenous and non-Indigenous peoples. The course will also guide students to begin to understand the diversity and depth of Indigenous societies, worldviews, and knowledge through a multi-disciplinary lens.
Semester 2
Delivery: Hybrid
Academy Salon I
HSTL  10
Units/ Hours: 120
Students are now exposed to the real-world experience in the college's innovative Academy Salon, further developing professional skills of introductory shape, colour and session styling in a safe supportive learning environment. During this practicum, students will work on clients and participate in creating an exceptional salon experience, from the initial greeting, consultation, preparation, service, and client aftercare. The students will be continually assessed on time management, communication, team building and practical skills.
Pre-Requisites
Foundations of Session Styling I (HSTL   2)
Foundations of Colour I (HSTL   3)
Foundations of Shape I (HSTL   1)
Customer Care and Retail Expertise
BUSN 264
Units/ Hours: 30
Students will continue to learn in-depth product knowledge and communication skills to become trusted professionals who educate and advise clients on maintaining healthy scalp and hair, as well as, recommending products to assist in achieving a variety of in-salon styles at home. Students will be taught how to cultivate the relationship between the stylist, the client, and the brand to highlight the importance of retailing, and client retention to build a successful profitable business. Knowledge will be shown through theoretical assessments.
Pre-Requisites
Product Knowledge and Customer Care (HSTL   4)
Intermediate Colour II
HSTL   8
Units/ Hours: 105
Broadening colour theory and palette, the students will be introduced to advanced colouring, and lightening techniques, including colour correction. Students will continue to develop skills in creative colour artistry, such as special effects and vibrant hues, using customized placement and tonal palette. Through hands-on learning, theoretical knowledge will be transferred into the practical labs where students will continue to build confidence in their hair colouring journey.
Pre-Requisites
Foundations of Colour I (HSTL   3)
Intermediate Session Styling II
HSTL   7
Units/ Hours: 60
Students become more fluent in the art of dressing hair, with a newly refined eye for beauty and balance. Learn the ways of wigs, extensions, and accessories to increase their creativity toolbox adapting to the ever-changing seasons of hair fashion. Students will build confidence and skill ability in creating memories for grad, prom, and wedding. Students will learn Mood-Board making and team collaboration with peers to prepare themed collections. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Pre-Requisites
Foundations of Session Styling I (HSTL   2)
Intermediate Shape II
HSTL   6
Units/ Hours: 105
Artistic sculpting will be taught to optimize angles of concave and convex and C-shape graduation. Further skills of scissor-over-comb, travelling layers, texturizing, and face framing will continue to elevate the students' technical abilities in the art of shaping. Students will develop further blow-drying and finishing techniques. Through hands-on learning, theoretical knowledge will be transferred into the practical labs for students to continue to develop all skills. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Pre-Requisites
Foundations of Shape I (HSTL   1)
Multicultural Hair and Cultural Sensitivity
HSTL   9
Units/ Hours: 45
Exploring various hair profiles, students will learn strategies to interact with different cultures to create an environment of inclusivity, while appreciating the multitude of hair textures. Students will learn that harsh chemicals are not required to create structural change to the hair. Learn how to transform hair texture using a permanent formaldehyde-free service to de-frizz and de-kink hair. Students will also be instructed in the methods of traditional permanent wave. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.

The theory of colour formulation will assist students to build confidence in achieving consistent results in lifting or depositing colour through a variety of techniques to colour hair using temporary, semi-permanent, demi-permanent, or permanent colour. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Foundations of Session Styling I
HSTL   2
Units/ Hours: 75
The craft of hairdressing goes beyond the salon, where scissors are rarely required. This is the world of editorial, film, fashion, bridal, and avant-garde. In this course, students will study the different genres of dressing hair, placements, braiding, techniques, tools, and the use of various pins and supportive products.  
Learn the famous beach waves, runway ponytail, classic chignons and how to begin branding your signature as a finisher. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Foundations of Shape I
HSTL   1
Units/ Hours: 135
Shaping is the cornerstone of the hairstyling industry. Each stylist: a sculptor; the medium: hair. Students will learn in-depth consultations techniques with awareness to, hair texture, lifestyle, and face shape. This course introduces the student to the art of interior and exterior shaping with the underpinning knowledge to distribution and parting hair. Students will learn foundational layering, framing, and blow-drying methods. Through hands-on learning, theoretical knowledge will be transferred into the practical labs. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Guest Artist Experience
HSTL   5
Units/ Hours: 45
This course introduces students to the hairstyling industry from both guest and artist perspectives, emphasizing the importance of effective communication and the unique journey of hairstylists. Students will explore the techniques and stories of industry experts, gaining valuable insights into the art of hairstyling and meaningful connections. Learning activities include research, role-playing, and a combination of theoretical and practical assessments.
Marketing, Branding and Social Media

Units/ Hours: 30
Self-promotion, marketing, and retail sales in the spa industry is key to having a successful business. Knowledge and skills will be shown through theoretical and practical assessments.
Product Knowledge and Customer Care
HSTL   4
Units/ Hours: 30
Understanding ingredients and the formulations of products is a key component in the journey to becoming a successful hairstylist. Students will learn how the selection of professional products plays an integral role throughout shape, colour and session styling to create the hair design. Throughout this course the student will learn the importance of the following topics: client-focused approach to retailing, the importance of using hair care products that support ethical and sustainable farming practices, natural versus synthetic, and the ethics of using cruelty-free products. Knowledge will be shown through theoretical assessments.
Introduction to Indigenous Studies
INDG  49

Students will be fully immersed in all daily business activities, with a focus on client rapport building, retention, retail expertise and overall exceptional client experiences. Business Planning and Entrepreneurship
BUSN 265
Units/ Hours: 30
Sole proprietorship is a common business model in the hairstyling industry. Students will learn the importance of financial planning, facilities, inventory management, employment law, and insurance considerations. This course will teach the basics of day-to-day salon operations including sensible and ethical decision making. Assessments include a full business plan that can be used to apply for funds to turn your goals into a reality.
Makeup Artistry
HSTL  14
Units/ Hours: 45
Makeup is the magical art of transformation. Students will learn the techniques of concealing, contouring, shading and colour placement to achieve a flawless canvas to showcase, day, evening, bridal and mature makeup application. Students will be encouraged to let their creativity soar with more advanced skills to create stunning images and illusions for film, photographic, avant garde and runway showpieces. By the end of the semester, projects will culminate in a comprehensive portfolio to showcase their work. Knowledge and skills will be shown through practical assessments.
