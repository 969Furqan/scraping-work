Our music degree programs have been carefully designed to offer students a fine balance between academic and performance courses. As a student, you will explore the various dimensions of music through courses taught by teachers of national and international stature as well as receive mentorship opportunities from seasoned musicians and professionals in the field.
As a music student, you will have the opportunity to take courses in:
Performance
Theory
Conducting
Composition
Orchestration
Canadian Music
World Music
Pedagogy
It is our goal to develop your talent as both a soloist and a collaborative musician, and cultivate your creativity by exposing you to diverse musical genres, traditions, and innovations. Instruction is available in all orchestral instruments as well as:
Classical Guitar
Piano
Voice
Saxophone
Organ
You will have the opportunity to participate in a variety of music ensembles such as:
Vocal Ensemble
Chamber Ensemble
Wind Ensemble
New Music Ensemble
Jazz Ensemble
Our strong community connections open the door to academic and professional opportunities with the Thunder Bay Symphony Orchestra, New Music North, Consortium Aurora Borealis, and more. You will be able to share your talent and develop your professional skills as you progress through the program.
The Lakehead Advantage
Our well-rounded approach to music education sets our Department of Music apart from other programs across Canada in that you are required to touch on every aspect of music throughout your undergraduate studies, from performance to musicology, composition, conducting and more. This breadth of study allows you to discover and develop hidden talents and abilities.
Our department is small and conducive to an interactive and intimate learning environment. All course are taught by our renowned faculty – setting the stage for academic excellence and professionalism.
The A.L. Musselman Music Library is located in the William H. Buset Centre for Music and Visual Arts and is open to all students. The library includes a large number of musical scores, recordings, and resources to support any project or personal interest in the field of music.
Transfer Options
It’s easy to transfer to Lakehead.
Our pathway and general transfer policies are designed to help you maximize your credits from your current or previous studies at either college or university – or even both.
There are a number of ways to transfer to Lakehead. Let us help you achieve your degree completion goals. For more information regarding specific admission requirements from college or university, check out the “Admissions Requirement” tab above and select your most recent level of study.
Applying to Lakehead University is easy and we are here to help you along the way.

For a balanced education, additional elective credits are available within the Faculty of Social Sciences and Humanities.
As an undergraduate student, you will attend your in-class lectures, one-on-one music lessons with an instructor, performance/ensemble course(s), and plan your practice time either on campus or at home.
Our professors are always available for advice and have an open-door policy. Due to the small size of our department, we encourage you to get to know your peers and professors. We host a variety of social events throughout the year to promote community spirit.
The William H. Buset Centre for Music and Visual Arts is home to the Jean McNulty Recital Hall, the A.L. Musselman Music Library, classrooms and teaching studios, as well as various practice modules equipped with instruments – all of which are available for student use.
The Lakehead University Music Association (LUMA) is an official student club dedicated to increasing music awareness within the university and the community. LUMA hosts a variety of activities throughout the year including social events, student concerts, fundraisers, and music trips, as well as sponsors the esteemed LUMINA Concert Series.
In your third and fourth year, you will have the option to enroll in advanced music courses such as Musicology, Conducting, Orchestration and Composition to enrich your undergraduate experience and prepare yourself for diverse professional opportunities in the future.
